Types of Financial Aid

Scholarships
Merit-based awards based on academic achievement, leadership, and talents. No repayment required.
  • Academic Excellence Scholarship: $5,000/year
  • Leadership Award: $3,000/year
  • Departmental Scholarships available
Grants
Need-based aid that doesn't need to be repaid. Based on financial need determined by FAFSA.
  • Pell Grant: Up to $7,395/year
  • Bethel Need-Based Grant: $2,000-$10,000/year
  • State Grants available
Loans
Low-interest federal loans with flexible repayment options. Subsidized loans don't accrue interest while in school.
  • Federal Direct Subsidized: 4.53% interest
  • Federal Direct Unsubsidized: 5.05% interest
  • PLUS Loans for parents

How to Apply for Financial Aid

FAFSA - Free Application for Federal Student Aid

The FAFSA is the first step in applying for federal, state, and institutional financial aid. It's free and determines your eligibility for grants, loans, and work-study.

Key Deadlines
  • FAFSA Opens: October 1
  • Priority Deadline: March 1
  • Bethel Scholarship Deadline: February 15
